It's All About The Process

  1. Initial Questionnaire - My process begins with research. I explore your business, your product - whether it’s an existing piece of software, or simply an idea, and your visions for the future through a straight-forward questionnaire. My questionnaire is designed to ensure that we’re a good match for each other, and to quickly bring me up to speed on your project.

  2. Follow-up Call - The next step is to dive into the concrete details of your product. When stripped to its core, what is the purpose of your application? In tangible terms, what will it look like, and how will it operate? How will your users interact with it? In what ways will it be improved and extended in the future? We’ll discuss these details and more on a follow-up call.

  3. Proposal, Timeline & Contract - Next, I’ll draft the ideas we discussed into a proposal and put together an estimated timeline for completion. This proposal and timeline will be sent over for review along with a contract outlining our agreement.

  4. Development - After the contract has been signed and returned along with an initial deposit and any other necessary assets and resources, the development process begins. During development, I like to give each client my full attention by only focusing on a single project per day. This level of focus and dedication helps ensure that I deliver the highest quality possible to my clients. Expect weekly updates during the development phase outlining my progress and plans for the upcoming week.

  5. Internal QA & Review - Quality assurance and testing happens throughout the development process, but at the end of each development cycle I devote time specifically to thorough hands-on testing of the application before I send it out for review.

  6. Staging & Acceptance - If possible, your application will be deployed to a private staging server. You’ll be invited to use the application on this staging server and go through any acceptance criteria you may have.

  7. Final Delivery - After acceptance on the staging server and payment of final invoice, the final development assets will be delivered to you. Depending on the project, this may mean the handing over of a Git repository, deployment to a production server, or both.
